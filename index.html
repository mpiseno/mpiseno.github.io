Bio

I work on systems for generalized robotic intelligence. I am currently focused on learning task representations for planning and manipulation. Mainly interested in humanoids and leveraging internet-scale multi-modal data for representation learning.

I work in the Hybrid Robotics Lab at UC Berkeley advised by Koushil Sreenath. I obtained my MS from Stanford University advised by Karen Liu where I worked on robust locomotion and motion tracking for physics-based character animation. I spent time at Robotics @ Google (now DeepMind) working on learning natural locomotion gaits for humanoid robots under Atil Iscen and Jason Peng. My research is supported by the National Defense Science and Engineering Graduate (NDSEG) Fellowship.
